Futures Trading

Not forex. Something bigger.

Futures are standardised, exchange-listed contracts to trade a financial asset at a set price on a future date. Unlike forex — which trades over-the-counter between brokers — futures trade on the CME Group (Chicago Mercantile Exchange), one of the world's most liquid, regulated markets.

When you buy one ES contract, you're controlling exposure to the S&P 500 index. When you buy one CL contract, you control 1,000 barrels of crude oil. The prices you see are the same prices institutions trade at — no broker markup, no re-quotes.

Micro contracts (MES, MNQ, MCL, MGC) are 1/10th the size of full contracts, giving you all the same markets with a fraction of the risk per tick. Available on every IPFX Futures account size.

Exchange-Listed & Regulated
Every trade clears through the CME Group — the same exchange used by hedge funds, banks, and institutional desks. No OTC risk, no broker counterparty.
Tick-Based P&L
Each market has a defined tick value. MES = $1.25/tick. ES = $12.50/tick. NQ = $5/tick. You always know exactly how much each move is worth.
Micro Contracts — Lower Risk, Same Markets
Trade MES instead of ES to get 1/10th the exposure. Perfect for managing risk on smaller accounts while still accessing the same CME liquidity.
Session-Based Trading
CME futures trade nearly 24 hours on weekdays. All positions must be closed by 4:00 PM CT (end of regular session). IPFX uses EOD trailing drawdown — not intraday — giving you maximum room to work.

FAQ

Questions answered.

Your drawdown floor locks in based on your account balance at the close of each trading session (4:00 PM CT). If you start at £25,000 with a £1,000 trailing drawdown, your floor is £24,000. If you close a session at £26,500, your floor rises to £25,500. Crucially — intraday peaks don't move it. If you're up £2,000 mid-session but close flat, your floor stays at £24,000. This is the most trader-friendly drawdown model available.

4:00 PM CT is the close of the CME regular trading session — when daily settlement prices are set. This is when EOD drawdown locks are calculated. Holding positions past session close introduces overnight gap risk and makes risk management significantly more complex. This is an industry-standard rule across all serious futures prop firms.
